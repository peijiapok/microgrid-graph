# 05 — Experimental Protocol

> Last revised 2026-04-22. How we prove the claims in `02_research_goal.md`. Every table in the paper must correspond to a section here, or the section is missing.

## 1. Topology splits

The new axis. Existing evidence trains and tests on the same feeder; that gives no transfer signal.

**G_train (in-family).** Feeders used during training. Held-out *scenarios* per feeder.

**G_ood (held-out topology).** Feeders *never* seen during training; held-out scenarios within each.

### Initial split (v1)

| Set | Feeder | Source | Size (approx n) |
|---|---|---|---|
| G_train | case33bw | pandapower | 32 loads |
| G_train | SimBench rural1 | SimBench | ~40 loads |
| G_train | SimBench rural2 | SimBench | ~60 loads |
| G_ood  | SimBench LV-urban | SimBench | ~100+ loads |
| G_ood  | IEEE 34-bus | pandapower | 34 |
| G_ood  | IEEE 123-bus | pandapower / OpenDSS converter | 123 |

Rationale: three G_train feeders give topology diversity (rural/urban/radial) without leaking the LV-urban and IEEE families used for OOD evaluation. LV-urban is already in the codebase as a held-out-by-accident feeder (the current paper tests on it; under the new protocol it becomes OOD).

### Split invariants

Any accepted split must satisfy:

1. No feeder appears in both G_train and G_ood.
2. At least one OOD feeder has |V| > max |V| in G_train (size-generalization).
3. At least one OOD feeder has topology class different from every G_train feeder (structural-generalization).

Splits are versioned in `configs/topology_split_vN.yaml` and referenced by hash in every result artifact.

## 2. Metrics

**Existing four (unchanged).**
- `weighted_served_energy`
- `unserved_critical_demand`
- `switching_count`
- `critical_continuity_ratio`

**New, required for the paper.**
- **`transfer_gap[metric]`** = (mean on G_ood) − (mean on G_train). Reported per metric.
- **`cvar_05[critical_continuity]`** = conditional value at risk at α=0.05 over held-out scenarios.
- **`feasibility_violations`** = count of (budget / cap / outage) breaches. Must be 0.
- **`wall_clock_per_step`** (CPU). Not a deployment target; a sanity check.

## 4. Ablations

### 4.1 Graph-necessity ablations

Goal: prove the graph is load-bearing, not decorative.

| Ablation | What is removed | Prediction | Kills review-killer |
|---|---|---|---|
| MLP | graph → MLP over concatenated features | transfer gap explodes | "why is this a graph paper" |
| Random edges | adjacency → Erdős–Rényi with same |E| | in-family metric degrades | same |
| Identity adjacency | adjacency → I | in-family metric degrades | same |

### 4.2 Objective ablations

| Ablation | What is removed |
|---|---|
| No continuity term | critical continuity-drop penalty disabled |
| No imitation | SmoothL1 teacher term off |
| No switching penalty | switching term off |
| No CVaR | replace CVaR-minimax (C4) with lexicographic selection |

### 4.3 Projection ablations

| Ablation | What is used |
|---|---|
| Softmax + normalization | no feasibility guarantee |
| Current iterative water-filler | loop-bounded |
| Closed-form projection (C3) | exact feasibility |

## 5. Compute budget

- **CPU smoke suite.** End-to-end on a single feeder (case33bw), single seed, reduced epochs. Target: <10 min on a laptop. Used as a CI-gate and as the collaborator's onboarding test.
- **Workshop-paper budget.** Full topology-split sweep × 3 seeds × 6 learned methods ≈ 18 training runs. Target: ~100 GPU-hours total on an A100 equivalent.
- **Main-conference budget.** Above + OOD families expanded + theory-matching experiments ≈ 300 GPU-hours.

If compute is tight, reduce in this order: seeds first, then ablations, then baselines. Never reduce the OOD topology set — that is the paper.

## 6. Reporting

Every table in the paper is populated from a single JSON under `results/` generated by the harness. No hand-typed numbers. A failing pipeline means the table is blank — that is deliberate.

| Paper table | Generator |
|---|---|
| Main result: methods × feeders × metrics | `scripts/report_main.py` (to write) |
| Transfer gap: methods × in-family vs OOD | `scripts/report_transfer.py` (to write) |
| Ablations: components × metrics | `scripts/report_ablations.py` (to write) |
| Feasibility: methods × violations | `scripts/report_feasibility.py` (to write) |

Scripts live in `scripts/` (to create). Tables land in `paper/tables/` as `.tex` fragments.

## 7. Seeds and determinism

- Each experiment seeded on (method, feeder, seed_idx). Three seeds minimum for any claim.
- PyTorch and numpy seeded at process start. Non-determinism from pandapower solvers is logged but not seeded (physics convergence is a separate invariant).

## 8. Physics audit

Post hoc. Every reported rollout is checked for pandapower convergence on a sample of scenarios. A rollout that fails physics convergence is reported separately, not silently dropped.
